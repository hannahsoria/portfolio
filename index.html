About Me

I am an artist, designer, technology enthusiast, and current master’s student at the University of Colorado Boulder, pursuing a degree in Creative Technology and Design at the ATLAS Institute. My interests span the intersection of art, design, and technology to create innovative works in response to the surrounding world and develop solutions to complex problems using empathetic design. I am interested in visualizing data in an aesthetic and informative manner to communicate outcomes in simple and engaging ways. I am also passionate about responsible and ethical computing and design to ensure safety, accessibility, and representation. I uphold this though being apart of the Responsible Computing Challenge Alumni Council supported by Mozilla Foundation and Mellon Foundation. I hold a BA in Computer Science and Studio Art, with a printmaking focus, from Colby College where I developed a strong foundation in technical and creative problem-solving.

Projects

Fred Again Concert Visuals

Concert visuals for Fred Again for his album Actual Life 3. This creates two screens: the concert visuals that a viewer would see, and the control panel that someone hidden from the crowd would use. The visuals interact with the music data through frequencies, amplitude, and waveform, which dictate the movement of the visuals. T visuals integrate with each other so you can set one visual, background, and text at a time creating a bunch of combinations.

Narcos Data Visualization

Visualization of data that corresponds to the show Narcos by displaying Medellín, the ratings for each episode as the points in the mountains, and the runtime for each episode is the height of the building.

Sudoku Solver

Sudoku game that creates a board and solves it by finding a value that is the only one in the row, column, and 3x3 section. The project creates cells, utilizes a 2D array, stack, and graphics to get the final board. A stack is then used when solving the board by storing the cells and keeping track of backtracking when it gets stuck.

Radial Basis Function Neural Network

Implementation of a neural network and use it to classify handwritten digits in the MNIST image dataset: the network will predict which numeric digit 0-9 is contained in test images that are presented to it. This will use radial basis functions (RBF) in the neural network hidden layer (RBF network).

Targeting Teens: Social Media Weapons Marketing

Authors: Hannah M. Soria; Stacy A. Doore

Presented at 2025 IEEE International Symposium on Ethics in Engineering, Science, and Technology, This paper explores the ethical concerns at the intersection of weapon advertising on Instagram targeting young users and the role of algorithm-driven content curation. This paper explores the ethical concerns at the intersection of weapon advertising on Instagram targeting young users and the role of algorithm-driven content curation. By analyzing the case study of the Uvalde shooting and the resulting lawsuits, we highlight the impact of exposure to social media weapons content on youth. We argue that new safeguards are needed to strengthen social media content moderation practices to protect vulnerable teen and young adult audiences.
